NBA Stats Viz

Visual Components for Basketball Game Text Broadcasting
In Progress

Design & Development Individual Project
My Role Visual concept, UI, UX
Tools Illustrator, Photoshop, GIMP, Framer

Design Goal / Feature Backlog

Literal Sports Live Broadcasting has long been a useful method for people who want to watch the games on the move, but it has transitioned to video broadcasting in the recent years. However, there still may be space fot simple text-based components on the screens. Is there a way to make these texts more lively and interesting? One of my findings is that most game watchers won't actually pay for the sports programs. Many sports fans either watch the games on TV at home or in the bars, or they go online to find a free live-streaming website, which, most of the time, is both full of ads and of bad image quality and streaming-speed.

  • Once click into a game, always clearly show team and active player names
  • Star Player Highlight / Matchup Comparisons
  • Team stats and comparisons
  • Play-by-play text and symbol live update
  • Easy interaction flow / swipe and tap
  • Easy switch between live game, stats, and game list
  • Fun / playful / design-y

Existing Component Analysis

Common components to show stats and game progress of basketball games currently include:

  • Timeline of play-by-play actions
  • Tables of names, stat titles, and numbers
  • Line charts tracking lead changes between the two teams
  • Bar or pie charts showing final stat comparisons
  • Team leaders (profile pictures) of main categories of stats
  • Video highlights as of current game progress


While bar / line charts and pie charts are generally visually uninteresting, the brand colors make them even less friendly and sometimes overwhelming to look at. The shot chart (showing the court) and the lead changing diagram, which are considered classic apparatuses for the sports, cannot hold so much information with such big use of space on the page. For such detailed graphs, they almost only give the audience an initial visual impact, and that will be it.
