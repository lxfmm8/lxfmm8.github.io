Paper Thingy

DIY Paper Building Blocks for All Creatives

Type Individual Thesis Project for MFA
My Role Product Concept, Branding, Illustrations, UI, UX
Tools HTML, CSS/Sass, Javascript, AngularJS, Illustrator, Photoshop, GIMP, Blender 3D, Silhouette Studio

Thesis Statement

Building blocks remain an excellent early learning tool, allowing children to develop spatial relation skills as well as learning to collaborate, communication, and create.Â How can the concept of blocks be updated for more sophisticated play? Paper Thingy proposes a system for folding complex objects out of 2D paper templates. The templates come in sets of interlocking forms that can be combined to create multi-dimensional structures.


Key Concept

Dynamic Modularity - My key concept is to create an array of building blocks that is diverse and dynamic in terms of geometrical forms so that people enjoy the process of making and building modular structure. With a big set of different forms and structures, I can figure out what are the different needs for different groups of people: paper craft lovers, architects, designers, kids, etc.


Design Process


Play Testings

Through testing in different environments / DIY events / neighborhoods, I gained experience observing players of different genders and ages interacting / modifying / destroying certain pieces of the set, from which I learned where people needed more instructions and what modules to include for people at different levels of playing with paper and glue.


Play Testings Round 2

Play testing / demo during Maker Faire, in a more formal, almost museum like setting. Children were accompanied by their parents, and they were more careful with the blocks.


Findings & Instruction Design

After several rounds of testing and iterating, I found that teaching people about how to building pieces and best practices are essential to the product's success, so I made a couple of different styles of instructions based on my 3D modeled graphics.
