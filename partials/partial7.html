Paper Thingy

Toy Design

Directors: Anezka Sebek, Kyle Li
Tools: HTML, CSS/Sass, Javascript, AngularJS, Illustrator, Photoshop, GIMP, Blender 3D, Silhouette Studio
Documentation: xiaofenglinthesis1.wordpress.com
Year: 2016

Building blocks remain an excellent early learning tool, allowing children to develop spatial relation skills as well as learning to collaborate, communication, and create.Â How can the concept of blocks be updated for more sophisticated play? Paper Thingy proposes a system for folding complex objects out of 2D paper templates. The templates come in sets of interlocking forms that can be combined to create multi-dimensional structures.

Play Testings:

Through testing in different environments / DIY events / neighborhoods, I gained experience observing players of different genders and ages interacting / modifying / destroying certain pieces of the set, from which I learned where people needed more instructions and what modules to include for people at different levels of playing with paper and glue.

Construction Manuals:

These construction manuals are created using Adobe Illustrator to come with the template packages.



Exhibition Installation:

My thesis exhibition demonstrates Paper Thingy in a mixed fashion of toy, lifestyle, and e-commerce. On the wall and three Muji tables are decorative configurations, online store, core set modules, package designs, test empty templates, ready-made prototypes, instructions, along with promoting product flyers and cards.
